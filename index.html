Inspiration

Awarded by the DM for playing out the character's personality traits.

When used, gives advantage on an attack roll, saving throw, or ability check.

Concentration

You can only have a single active concentration spell at a time.

If you take damage, you must succeed a Con saving throw (DC 10 or half the damage taken) or lose concentration.

Dying or falling unconscious ends concentration immediately.

Group Checks

When players are trying to accomplish something as a group.

Everyone in the group makes the ability check. If at least half the group succeeds, the whole group succeeds. Otherwise, the group fails.

Death Saving Throws

When a player starts their turn with 0 HP, they make a death saving throw.

The player rolls a D20, and the outcome is determined by the table below. On three successes, the character becomes stabile. On three failures, the character dies.

d20 Outcome
1 Two failures
2-9 One failure
10-19 One success
20 Regain 1 HP

If the player takes any damage at 0 HP, they gain one failure. If the damage is from a critical hit, they gain two failures. If the damage is greater than or equal to their maximum HP, they instantly die.

Exhaustion

If a character doesn't eat or drink, they suffer exhaustion.

Ability descriptors

It is often useful to describe NPCs not by their actual abilities ("he is very strong", "he is not very intelligent"), but by using descriptors that give a better picture of the character.

d6 High Ability
1 Strength - powerful, brawny, strong as an ox
2 Dexterity - lithe, agile, graceful
3 Constitution - hardy, hale, healthy
4 Intelligence - studious, learned, inquisitive
5 Wisdom - perceptive, spiritual, insightful
6 Charisma - persuasive, forceful, born leader

d6 Low Ability
1 Strength - feeble, scrawny
2 Dexterity - clumsy, fumbling
3 Constitution - sickly, pale
4 Intelligence - dim-witted, slow
5 Wisdom - oblivious, absentminded
6 Charisma - dull, boring
